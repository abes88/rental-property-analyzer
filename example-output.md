## 9. Final Investment Verdict

**Meets cash flow neutral requirement: No**

The property fails by a wide margin (-$1,287/mo) and cannot be structured to break even under any realistic scenario.

### Primary Strengths
- **Strong area fundamentals** — Reston has high incomes ($139K median), tech employer base, Silver Line metro, excellent schools
- **Below-market price/sqft** — at ~$238/sqft vs $442/sqft Reston condo average, there may be a value play if the unit is renovated
- **Utilities included in HOA** — simplifies rental management and reduces tenant disputes

### Primary Risks
- **$899/month in HOA + RA fees** — consumes 36% of rent before any other expense; this is the deal-breaker
- **1972 construction** — special assessment risk is high for aging building systems (roof, plumbing, HVAC)
- **4BR condo is a niche product** — families with 4BR budgets prefer houses; tenant pool is limited
- **STR not viable** — Fairfax County primary residence requirement eliminates this optionality

### What Would Make This Deal Work

| Lever | Required | Realistic? |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Purchase price for CF neutral (at current rent) | ~$100,000 | **No** — not possible in this market |
| Rent needed for CF neutral (at current price) | $4,138/mo | **No** — 48% above market |
| HOA needed for CF neutral (at current rent/price) | ~$250/mo total | **No** — condo association controls this |
| All cash purchase (no mortgage) | Eliminates $1,783 P&I; CF = +$496/mo | Maybe — but $357K cash for $496/mo (1.67% return) is terrible use of capital |

**The structural problem is the $899/month HOA + RA burden on a mid-priced condo.** No financing structure, rent optimization, or management efficiency can overcome it. This is a pass.

If you're targeting Reston for investment, look for townhomes or SFH with RA-only fees ($816/yr) and no condo fee — that removes ~$10K/year from the expense side and fundamentally changes the math.
